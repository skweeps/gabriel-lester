WHERE SPIRITS DWELL

With this series of constructions, Lester creates a false breeze through a wood cabin. Despite the absence of the breeze, the fabric is locked in place. The cabin is lit by a spotlight from all angles other than above. The lighting can be switched to come from inside the house, or be projected onto the house, drawing drastically different emotions with each lighting change. The piece was exhibited at the ‘19th Sydney Benniale’ curated by Juliana Engberg. Lester sought to evoke elements of the supernatural and isolation

Where Spirits Dwell; Physical Structures; 2014

Lester has said he wanted to create a real life freeze frame, and I believe he achieved exactly what he wanted to. The cabin was described by viewers as something that exists between space and time. It stood out to me because it reminded me of the location “the Black Lodge '' from the TV show ‘Twin Peaks’, another location that exists between space and time.
