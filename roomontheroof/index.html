The ROOM On The Roof

Room on the roof; Sculpture and display; 2015

The work itself is a sculpted city skyline placed atop a conveyer belt on an elevated desk. The conveyor belt itself is in constant rotation, transitioning from a forested park to an urban landscape. The sculpture itself however is only half the installation, as there are three large lights projecting the sculpture's shadow onto the chosen backdrop. The display itself is the piece, with the shadows dancing and glimmering on the wall behind. He described this piece as a “skyline of a recurring dream”

Due to the shifting of the lights, the piece is destined to an infinite loop, changing ever so slightly each time to take on new forms. The merge of nature and city was displayed in the streets of Amsterdam to remind passers-by of the everlasting infiniteness of modern city life.
